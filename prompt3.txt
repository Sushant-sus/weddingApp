# Part A (v2) — Design Brief: Event Planning + Service Marketplace ("Liquid Glass")

> Paste this whole block into a fresh Claude design/artifact chat. It is self-contained.
> Goal: mock up a mobile app (Flutter target) where people plan their own events **and** hire service providers through a request-for-quote marketplace, all in an iOS-26 "Liquid Glass" visual language. Produce clean, reproducible screens + a component sheet, light **and** dark mode, with exact glass values noted so they can be rebuilt in Flutter.

---

## 1. Product in one line

A celebration/event planner with a built-in vendor marketplace: a **host** builds an event itinerary, tags items that need a service (e.g. a *Mehendi* ceremony needs a mehendi artist), opens that need to **providers**, and providers respond with a **price pitch**. The host compares pitches and books.

It is **two-sided** but one app: a person can be a Host, a Provider, or both. Design must make switching between the two modes feel effortless.

---

## 2. The core loop (design around this — it's the whole point)

1. Host creates an event → builds an **itinerary** (e.g. Engagement, Mehendi, Sangeet, Wedding, Reception).
2. On any itinerary item, host taps **"Need a service"** → picks a category (Mehendi, Catering, Photography, Decor, Makeup, DJ/Dhol, Pandit/Officiant, Mandap, Venue, Transport…).
3. Host posts a **Service Request** for that item. Two paths, both must be designed:
   - **Broadcast** — open to all matching providers in the area.
   - **Targeted** — host browses providers and invites specific ones.
4. Providers see the request in their **inbox/feed** and submit a **Pitch**: price (string-formatted money), a short message, and availability for the date.
5. Host sees all pitches on the request, **compares** them (price, rating, portfolio, distance), and **selects/books** one.
6. Booked item shows on the itinerary with the provider attached; other pitches are auto-declined. Optional: chat + review after the event.

Design the empty, one-pitch, and many-pitches versions of the request-detail screen.

---

## 3. Roles & permissions (gate the UI by these)

- **Owner** — created the event; full control, can delete, can manage members and money.
- **Leader / Organizer** — can edit itinerary, post/accept service requests, see costs.
- **Member / Guest** — view itinerary, RSVP, contribute to gifts; **cannot** see the Costs tab or post requests.
- **Provider** — separate hat; sees provider dashboard, requests addressed to them/their categories, their own pitches, bookings, earnings. Has no access to a host's private cost data.

Reflect gating visually: the **Costs** tab is hidden entirely for Member/Guest, and the host's cost figures are never shown to providers. Show a tasteful locked/empty state where a role lacks access.

---

## 4. Visual language — "Liquid Glass" (iOS 26)

- Frosted, translucent **glass cards and sheets** floating over a soft gradient.
- A floating **pill-shaped translucent bottom nav** with thin-line, semi-transparent icons and a **liquid active-tab highlight** (a soft blurred accent blob that animates under the selected tab).
- Layered depth: gradient background → blurred glass surfaces → crisp content/text on top.
- Light **and** dark mode, both first-class. Glass is lighter fill in light mode, darker fill in dark mode; gradient shifts from champagne-bright to deep plum-night.
- Motion: gentle, liquid, spring-based. Tab highlight morphs between positions; sheets rise with a soft blur ramp.
- Mode switch (Host ⇄ Provider) is a glass segmented control or a profile-menu toggle — must feel like flipping a card, not navigating away.

---

## 5. Palette & type

**Gradient (behind the glass):** soft rose → champagne → lavender (light); deep plum → wine → indigo (dark). Per-event-type accent allowed (e.g. wedding = rose-gold, corporate = slate-teal, birthday = coral), but keep one shared neutral glass system so the app feels coherent.

**Suggested tokens (designer may refine — keep them reproducible):**
- Accent / rose-gold: `#C9A28A` · Champagne: `#F4E7DA` · Lavender: `#CDC1E6` · Plum (dark bg): `#2A1B33`
- Success `#6FBF8E` · Warning `#E0A458` · Error `#D9737A` · Info `#7FA8D9`
- Text on glass (light): `#2B2630` @ 92% · (dark): `#F6F1EC` @ 92%

**Typography:** an elegant **serif for display/headings** (event titles, screen headers — e.g. a high-contrast didone/transitional serif) paired with a clean **sans for UI/body** (labels, numbers, nav). Money/stat numbers in tabular sans. Keep it wedding-elegant but not fussy.

## 9. Data the marketplace implies (for the designer's awareness)

Money fields render as **strings** (e.g. `"12,500"`), not floats — design fields/labels accordingly. New entities behind the screens: `ServiceCategory`, `ServiceRequest` (status: open/closed/booked), `Pitch` (price, message, availability, status), `ProviderProfile` (portfolio, pricing, service area, rating), `Booking`. Itinerary categories are a fixed set of 6, each with its own color — keep those colors consistent across timeline, chips, and request screens.
